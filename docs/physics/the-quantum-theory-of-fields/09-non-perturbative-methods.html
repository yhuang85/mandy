9.1. Symmetry#

In this section, we’ll revisit various symmetry principles encountered in perturbation theory from a non-perturbative perspective.

First let’s consider the translation symmetry, which generates a \(4\)-momentum operator \(P_{\mu}\). According to Eq.6.3.14 we have

\[\left[P_{\mu}, O(x)\right] = \ifrak \frac{\p}{\p x^{\mu}} O(x)\]

for any operator \(O(x)\) that doesn’t explicitly involve spacetime coordinates. If the in- and out-states are also taken to have definite momenta in the following sense

\[P^{\mu} \Psi_{\alpha}^+ = p_{\alpha}^{\mu} \Psi_{\alpha}^+, \qquad P^{\mu} \Psi_{\beta}^- = p_{\beta}^{\mu} \Psi_{\beta}^-\]

then the time-ordered product amplitude considered in Gell-Mann and Low’s theorem Eq.5.2.18 satisfies the following differential equation

\[\begin{split}&(p_{\beta\mu} - p_{\alpha\mu})\left(\Psi_{\beta}^-, T\{O_a(x_1), O_b(x_2), \cdots\} \Psi_{\alpha}^+\right) \\ &\qquad= \left(\Psi_{\beta}^-, \left[P_{\mu}, T\{O_a(x_1), O_b(x_2), \cdots\}\right] \Psi_{\alpha}^+\right) \\ &\qquad= \ifrak \left(\frac{\p}{\p x_1^{\mu}} + \frac{\p}{\p x_2^{\mu}} + \cdots\right) \left(\Psi_{\beta}^-, T\{O_a(x_1), O_b(x_2), \cdots\} \Psi_{\alpha}^+\right)\end{split}\]

The general solution to the equation is given as follows

\[\left(\Psi_{\beta}^-, T\{O_a(x_1), O_b(x_2), \cdots\} \Psi_{\alpha}^+\right) = e^{\ifrak(p_{\alpha}-p_{\beta}) \cdot x} F_{ab\cdots}(x_1-x_2, x_1-x_3, \cdots)\]

where \(x = c_1x_1 + c_2x_2 + \cdots\) as long as \(c_1 + c_2 + \cdots = 1\). Moreover \(F_{ab\cdots}\) can be any function that depends only on the coordinate differences.
